## Gratitudes

<!-- Why this has its own section: gratitude prompting was one of the first things that got dropped
during check-ins because it conflicted with other coaching context (e.g., "don't triage daily
basics individually" + "lead with the actionable stuff"). Without an explicit callout, Claude
will skip it. If something matters to you but keeps getting skipped, give it its own section so it doesn't get deprioritized by other instructions.
-->

Daily gratitude practice is one of the few "soft" interventions with solid research behind it. Emmons & McCullough found that people who wrote down three things they were grateful for weekly reported more optimism, more exercise, and fewer physical complaints. Wood et al. found that gratitude journaling before bed improved sleep quality. The mechanism isn't toxic positivity — it's attention-shifting. Training yourself to *notice* what's already working. Three specific, real things beat vague "I'm grateful for my life."

Sources: [Emmons & McCullough, 2003](https://doi.org/10.1037/0022-3514.84.2.377) · Wood et al., 2009

## Daily Coaching Checklist

*Complete every step when updating today's daily note during a check-in. Customize to your needs.*

1. **Weather.** Fill in the forecast.
2. **Things I Want To Do Today.** Fill in based on the check-in conversation.
3. **Carry forward "Things for tomorrow" from yesterday.**
4. **Dinner.** Fill in from the weekly cooking plan if applicable.
5. **Calendar.** Add any appointments.
6. **"Make tomorrow easier" section.** Surface prep actions for tomorrow.
7. **File yesterday's daily note** into the monthly archive.
8. **Verify.** Scan the full daily note top to bottom. Every section should be touched.
